Our Plating & Coating Services

We offer a wide range of surface finishes to enhance durability, corrosion resistance, and appearance.

Zinc Plating

Also known as galvanization, this process provides excellent corrosion resistance and enhances the aesthetic appearance of metal surfaces.

Zinc-Iron Plating

A zinc-iron alloy is electroplated onto a substrate, offering superior corrosion resistance compared to standard zinc plating, especially in high-temperature environments.

Nickel (Ni) Plating

A layer of nickel is deposited onto a metal substrate, providing excellent wear resistance, hardness, and a decorative finish.

Ni-Cr Plating

Nickel-chromium plating involves applying a layer of nickel for brightness and corrosion resistance, followed by a thin layer of chromium for a hard, mirror-like finish.

Tin Plating

Known as tinning, this process deposits a thin layer of tin to provide excellent conductivity, solderability, and corrosion resistance, ideal for electronics.

Copper Plating

Used as an underplate to improve adhesion for other coatings or as a final finish for its high conductivity and distinctive appearance.

Silver Plating

A thin layer of silver is deposited to provide superior electrical conductivity, low contact resistance, and excellent solderability for high-performance applications.

Powder Coating

A dry finishing process that provides a durable, versatile, and environmentally friendly finish, more resistant to chipping and scratching than traditional paint.

Black Oxidation

Also known as blackening, this chemical process creates a blackened finish on ferrous metals for mild corrosion resistance and minimal dimensional change.

Gold Plating

A thin layer of gold is deposited for exceptional corrosion resistance, conductivity, and reliability, used in critical electronic and aerospace components.
